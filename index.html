    • A Guide for Using a Debit Card at an ATM


    You may use your debit card for more than just in-person and online purchases. Moreover, you can use it to withdraw money from ATMs (ATMs). Some people only use their debit cards to make cash withdrawals. You can use an ATM to withdraw money, deposit money, check your balance, and more. All of those tasks, with the exception of deposits and withdrawals, can also be completed online. Keep in mind the following

    Safety first

    Check to see if using an ATM is safe before you do so. Use ATMs only in pristine, well-lit areas. Thieves occasionally wait around for individuals who have plainly just withdrew cash. Check the ATM for any suspicious-looking items. On ATMs, thieves can install covert skimming equipment. These tools are capable of stealing your card information, and there may even be covert cameras nearby that can catch you entering your PIN. Use another ATM if anything seems off.

    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) What should I do since the ATM didn't give me money?

    Make sure to note the time and location of any ATM failures that prevent you from receiving the requested amount of cash. Frequently, a defect is detected by the system itself, so you won't need to take any action to correct it. However, you might have to get in touch with the bank and submit a claim.

    Is it secure to use the ATM to deposit money?

    Although problems may occasionally happen, ATMs are generally secure and trustworthy for depositing money into your account. It is advised that you make the deposit with a teller if it is significant in order to prevent any errors.
